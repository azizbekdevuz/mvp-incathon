# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want collaboration on **Foreign NPC** / **mvp-incathon** to stay constructive. The project is deliberately silly on the surface (Konglish, NPC roasts, “survival” copy) — humor belongs **in the product and in the writing**, not at the expense of real people.

We pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

**Positive examples**

- Giving and accepting actionable feedback on code, UX, and copy  
- Admitting mistakes and moving on  
- Respecting that this is a **hackathon-weight** codebase: pragmatic scope, no shame for “MVP” shortcuts if they’re honest  

**Unacceptable examples**

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks (including in issue threads or PR reviews)  
- Sexualized language or unwelcome attention  
- Publishing others’ private information  
- Sustained disruption of discussion or reviews  

Humor in **scenario text** or **UI microcopy** is fine when it punches up or stays fictional; don’t use “it’s just a joke” to excuse targeting contributors.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces: GitHub issues, PRs, Discussions, and any community channels you add later.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions (comments, commits, issues, PRs) that violate this code, or temporarily or permanently ban participants whose behavior is inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.

Reports: open a **private** security-sensitive contact path if you add one; until then, use GitHub **Security advisories** only for security bugs, and for conduct concerns prefer reaching maintainers you trust or filing a minimal **issue** asking for maintainer contact (without public accusation details if that would cause harm — GitHub’s tools are imperfect; do your best).
